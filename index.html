FutureHack is organizing the first ever Hackathon during the World Economic Forum in a call to action in support of solutions for of the United Nations SDGs. #FutureHack

FutureHack takes place in Davos/Klosters and in Slovenia offering the first Hackathon in conjunction with the Slovenian government as it aims to systemize community systems and governance structures on blockchain while also establishing and promoting an already thriving culture for decentralized projects and communities within Slovenia.

We're looking for those who are up for the challenge to bring emerging, distributed and decentralized solutions to solve key challenges: from climate change to healthcare we want your vision on how to tackle the worlds' most pressing issues. The hackathon kicks off a series of #FutureHack events including a virtual hackathon that launches after the World Economic Forum event.

Top minds and talent from the Blockchain community in Slovenia and around the world will gather at the World Economic Forum, and FutureHack aims to show how technology can solve Humanity’s greatest challenges. The hackathon focuses on Social Impact and Partnerships as solutions for how blockchain projects can solve for the UN SDGs and the future of humanity.

Enter today to participate in the world’s first Hackathon at the World Economic Forum and for the chance to win up to $500,000 in prizes!

FutureHack is seeking individuals and teams of top developers, designers, and entrepreneurs to team up together solve one of SIX pressing challenges that we are facing globally.

The Challenges

United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals

Health & Well-Being

Figure out solutions to address health issues such as creating affordable and scalable healthcare infrastructures, combating diseases effectively, and providing proper sanitation and health services to those without access.

Affordable & Clean Energy

Find affordable and scalable ways to increase accessibility to clean electricity for regions that lack energy distribution infrastructures.

Industry & Infrastructure

Find distributed ledger solutions to use industrial and infrastructural innovations to develop regions in environmentally sound and efficient ways.

Sustainable Cities & Communities

Increase urban populations accessibility to basic services, energy, housing, transportation, or create ways to improve upon infrastructures.

Climate Action

Find scalable solutions to reduce emissions, shift to cleaner economies run on renewable energy, and adapt to a changing climate.

Governance

Find ways to increase the effectiveness and accountability of current government institutions and imagine ways to design governance to work toward peace, inclusion, justice, and sustainable development.

Submit Your Own Challenge

Find innovative and creative ways to incorporate blockchain technology into solving today and tomorrows’ largest social issues.

The Judging Process

All teams will be required to present a short 3-minute demo of their project. Finalists from the Hackathon will be selected by a panel of World Virtual GovHack experts. Judges will be announced in the coming weeks.

Each submission will be scored in each round based on the following criteria with a minimum score of 0 and maximum score of 30 points, with the final score being the average of the judges’ scores.

Execution

Does the project successfully execute its intended technical goals? Does the project work-properly or as intended in its final presentation ready form? (5 points)

Impact

Does the project’s social impact line-up with the United Nations Sustainable Development goals? (5 points)

Creativity

Are the project’s concept and design unique or new solutions to its specific project impact goals?(5 points)

Blockchain

How successfully did the team integrate Blockchain technology into their solution or project? (5 points)

Scale

How scalable is the project within Blockchain and distributed systems as solutions for what’s needed now and in the future? (5 points)

Design

How well designed, including UX/UI and user flow, was the solution? How well were the project and ideas presented? (5 points)

The Rules

Team Size:

Teams of up to 5 participants are allowed. All team members must have completed the participation agreement to compete.

Fresh Code:

Teams may not begin code on this project until the competition officially begins. Please don’t come in and build on top of previous code for projects if you want to win. Teams may build on existing concepts, creative and ideas for projects and build fresh code as solutions for projects and initiatives that have been in development.

Ownership

Teams have full ownership of everything they build at our events and are free to do with it as they wish.
